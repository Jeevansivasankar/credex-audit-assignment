## Target User
The exact target user is a **VP of Engineering** or **CTO** at a Series A or Series B startup (20-150 employees). They are managing a growing engineering org and recently noticed their OpenAI/Anthropic/Cursor bills creeping up past $2k/mo, but they are too busy shipping product to deeply audit it themselves.

## What they do right before wanting this tool
They are reviewing their monthly Ramp or Brex statement, seeing line items for "OpenAI", "Anthropic", and "GitHub", and thinking: "Are my devs actually using all these seats? Could we consolidate?" Alternatively, they are searching Google for "Cursor vs Copilot enterprise pricing."

## Where they hang out
- **Online Communities:** CTO Craft Slack, the Heavybit community, specific subreddits like `r/SaaS` and `r/devops`.
- **Social:** Twitter (X) following lists curated around engineering leadership (e.g., following Gergely Orosz).
- **Newsletters:** Reading Pragmatic Engineer or ByteByteGo.

## Getting the first 100 users in 30 days ($0 budget)
I would build a targeted scrape of startups that recently announced Series A funding on TechCrunch. I would use Apollo/Clay to find their VP of Engineering and send a hyper-personalized cold email:
> "Hey [Name], saw the Series A, congrats. As you scale the eng team, your AI infra bill is going to explode. I built a free tool that audits your GitHub Copilot / OpenAI / Cursor stack to find redundant seats and cheaper tiers. Takes 30 seconds, no login required. Here's the link."
Additionally, I would launch the tool on Hacker News with a "Show HN" post titled: *Show HN: I built a tool to show startups how much they are overpaying for AI.*

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
Credex's unfair advantage is the **Audit Share Link**. When a CTO finds out they can save $1,200/mo, they don't keep it to themselves. They copy the share link and drop it in their `#engineering-leadership` Slack channel to show the CEO/CFO the potential savings. This creates a viral loop within the organization and potentially out to their peer networks.

If this works, week 1 looks like:
